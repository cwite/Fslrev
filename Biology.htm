Testing a leaf for Starch

Put the leaf in boiling water to break down cell membrane
Put it in a boiling tube with Ethanol inside in a beaker of boiling water
Put the leaf in warm water to remove the Ethanol
Put the leaf on a white tile and drop a few droplets of Iodine onto the leaf

Testing If Chlorophyll Is Necessary For Photosynthesis

Test Your leaf for Starch in the same manner as testing a leaf for starch

Conclusion

After testing The central Part of the leaf, the inside which contained Chlorophyll turned black with iodine whereas the outside remains white because there is no Chlorophyll.
This Shows us that Chlorophyll is Necessary for Photosynthesis.

Testing if light is Necessary for Photosynthesis

First cut a small piece of card out with an empty hole of a shape inside
Attach the peice of card to the leaf and leave in the sun for three days

Conclusion

The Card blocks the light and after testing for starch the part of the leaf not covered turns blue-black due to the presence of starch. Starch was only produced where the light got to.
This tells us that light is Necessary for Photosynthesis
